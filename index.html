Introduction to Korean Food - Bulgogi (Korean Grilled Beef)

I love Korean food. It might have something to do with the fact that I am a Korean. I was born and raised in Daejeon, South Korea. Even though my mom was a good cook, I didn't know much about cooking when I got married. Not only did I have to learn how to cook, but I also had to learn to cook American food for my husband who was born and raised in Midwest. He was not a very adventurous eater. I was thrilled to see my husband love the first Korean dish that I had ever made for him. I have yet to meet a non-Korean who doesn't like Bulgogi.


Bulgogi is one of classic Korean dishes where thinly sliced meat is marinated in a sweet and savory sauce made of soy sauce, sugar, and sesame oil, and grilled on a barbecue or on a stove-top griddle. Literally meaning "fire" and "meat", this well-known Korean dish has been in existence for nearly over a thousand years.



Intensely flavorful, it is undoubtedly one of the tastiest dish to put on the grill. When the marinated meat hit the sizzling skillet, you can immediately smell the tantalizingly sweet & smoky aroma fills the hot summer air. It is no doubt one of the most well-known Korean dishes to non-Koreans. It's definitely my husband's favorite Korean dish.
